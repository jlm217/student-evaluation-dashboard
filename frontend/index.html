Methodology Overview

This system uses a structured, AI-assisted approach designed to help make sense of a large number of student comments. The goal is to identify the key themes in student feedback so that course improvements can be made based on what students actually say—and mean.

The analysis is divided into four phases:

Phase 0: Data Cleaning and Preparation

Before any analysis begins, the some code helps (no AI in this phase) cleans and standardizes the raw data to ensure quality and consistency throughout the process.

This phase includes:

  • Column standardization: Maps different column names (e.g., "Question", "Q", "question") to consistent formats.
  • Text cleaning: Standardizes whitespace, encoding, and formatting issues that could interfere with analysis.
  • Duplicate detection: Identifies and handles potential duplicate responses.

Why this matters: Clean data ensures that the AI can focus on meaningful content rather than getting confused by formatting inconsistencies or unncessary additional information.

Phase 1: Turning Student Comments Into Codes

In this phase, the AI reads through student comments and applies a short descriptive label—or "code"—to each one. This process is similar to what a qualitative researcher would do manually: distill each comment down to the main idea or issue.

Here's how it works:

  • AI Model: Google Gemini 1.5 Flash processes each batch of student comments.
  • Batching for accuracy: Comments are analyzed in small groups of 20 to avoid overwhelming the system and keep processing costs reasonable.
  • Avoiding duplicates: When there are 30+ codes, the AI is shown recent codes to encourage reuse rather than creating variants like "Too much work" and "High workload" separately.
  • Sentiment tagging: Each comment is tagged as Positive, Negative, or Neutral based on tone.

Outcome: A set of unique codes are generated, each with a count and sentiment analysis.

Phase 2: Grouping Codes Into Themes

In this phase, the AI looks at the codes it has created and groups them into broader, more meaningful themes. This helps move from "what students say" to "what students are really talking about."

What guides this process:

  • AI Model: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash analyzes code patterns and creates thematic groupings.
  • Frequency matters: Codes that appear more often have stronger influence on theme formation.
  • Examples included: For each code, the AI reviews sample student quotes to understand deeper meaning.
  • Clear and Neutral themes: The AI is instructed to develop theme names that are descriptive and neutral (like "Assessment Feedback" or "TA Support") - avoiding emotional or overly complex labels.
  • Right number: Targets 6-10 themes based on data size for optimal manageability and clarity.
  • Quality check: Automatic verification ensures every code is assigned to exactly one theme.

Outcome: A focused set of themes are created, each with explanations and frequency data.

Phase 3: Data Integration and Executive Summary

Additional quantitative course data (schedules, grades) are merged with the themed comments to provide context and enable richer analysis.

The AI is instructed to:

  • AI Model: Google Gemini 2.5 Flash generates the executive summary and strategic recommendations.
  • Look for connections: The AI is prompted to act as an educational data strategist and produce a structured summary based on both the qualitative themes and the quantitative course performance data (e.g., DEW rates, grade distributions).
  • Find Strengths and Areas for Improvement: Performance data enables calculation of DEW rates and grade distributions.
  • Look for patterns: Highlight key patterns and contrasts between sections or modalities.
  • Emphasize student voices: The AI is instructed to use the direct student comments to inform and enhance its analysis.
  • Provide actionable insights: Suggest 2-3 concrete recommendations supported by both student voices and performance data.
